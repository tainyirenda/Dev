Informal Doesn’t Mean Invisible

Over 85% of Africa’s trade is informal—unseen, unsupported, and underserved. We’re building the infrastructure to change that. Powered by AI, grounded in Ubuntu. Afro Ubuntu TradeNet is a digital platform created to bring Africa’s invisible economy online and provide Africa with a bridge to global trade. We empower small businesses and traders across the continent by connecting them with opportunity, insight, and fairer trade through technology grounded in Ubuntu. The belief that we rise by lifting each other - “I am because we are”.

From Corner Shops to Global Trade

Informal doesn’t mean unintelligent. It means unseen, unsupported and undeserved. Afro Ubuntu TradeNet exists to rewrite that story. We go beyond POS digitisation, embedding AI-driven insight and ethical data practices, into the heart of informal trade. We are Africa-first, committed to digitising and decoding the invisible economy and dignifying trade. We are embedding AI, not to replace, but to reveal the power that lies in the hands of unregistered vendors, bustling dukas and spaza shop owners surviving day to day.

Our vision is to empower informal trade through inclusive, AI-powered infrastructure that is scalable across the continent, and to the world. We unlock credit through contextual data, build trust through transparent sourcing, and foster pan-african collaboration. From Whatsapp messages to credit scores, from inventory snapshots to economic insights. By 2028-2030, Afro Ubuntu TradeNet will be the leading trade intelligence layer for the world’s informal economies, connecting the African street vendor to global opportunity built on Ubuntu (“I am, because we are”).
